# Mechanical Design

## Overview
The mechanical system was designed to balance speed, stability, mass, and feasibility using the available project materials. The final vehicle used a three-wheel chassis with a widthwise baseplate orientation.

## Chassis Configuration
The final chassis used:

- Three-wheel layout
- Widthwise baseplate orientation
- Front driven axle
- Single rear wheel
- Meccano structural frame
- Cardboard ambulance-style body shell

The three-wheel configuration was chosen because it provided the best balance between feasibility, reduced rolling resistance, and reduced total mass.

## Drivetrain
The vehicle used a single DC motor mounted behind the front axle. Power was transferred to the front wheels through a pulley system.

### Mechanical Benefits
- Lower mass than a dual-motor drivetrain
- Reduced electrical complexity
- Improved speed potential
- Better use of available parts

### Mechanical Limitation
The pulley was slightly offset, which introduced torque steer and caused the vehicle to drift under acceleration. This was managed through careful starting alignment and Arduino timing adjustments.

## Frame and Mounting
The frame was built using Meccano parts, rods, bolts, spacers, and pulleys. The design prioritized rigidity while minimizing unnecessary material to stay under the mass limit.

## Body Design
The final exterior shell was an ambulance-inspired body called the “Ambearlance.” Cardboard was selected because it was lightweight, easy to cut, and simple to attach to the chassis.

Key body features:

- Hinged roof for access to electronics
- Wheel arches for front wheel clearance
- Recessed teddy bear seating area
- Reinforced seating area
- Side opening for launcher clearance

## Mechanical Lessons
The project demonstrated that small alignment issues can significantly affect autonomous vehicle performance. Future versions would improve pulley alignment, axle symmetry, and drivetrain balance.
