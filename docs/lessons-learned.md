# Lessons Learned

## Engineering Lessons

### Design Tradeoffs Matter
Throughout the project, every design decision involved balancing performance, weight, cost, reliability, manufacturability, and available resources.

### Testing Is Critical
Repeated testing was one of the most important factors contributing to project success. Many improvements were identified only after observing real-world behavior.

### Mechanical Alignment Affects Performance
The speed run demonstrated that small alignment issues, such as pulley offset and drivetrain geometry, can significantly affect autonomous vehicle performance.

### Simplicity Can Be Effective
The timing-based Arduino control strategy proved highly effective because the competition environment was fixed and repeatable.

### Safety Must Be Considered Early
Separating moving launcher components from the passenger area and electronics improved both safety and reliability.

### Cost Tracking Is Important
The project showed the value of monitoring costs throughout development rather than only at the end of the build process.

### Weight Management Should Be Continuous
Maintaining a low mass was critical for performance and competition requirements. Future projects should track subsystem mass throughout development.

## Team Lessons

- Open communication improved design quality.
- Multidisciplinary collaboration produced stronger solutions.
- Clear role allocation improved efficiency.
- Shared troubleshooting accelerated problem solving.

## Future Engineering Applications

The lessons learned from this project apply directly to:

- Robotics
- Mechatronics
- Autonomous systems
- Embedded systems
- Product development
- Mechanical design
- Engineering project management

## Summary
The Teddy Bear Wheelchair Project provided hands-on experience with the complete engineering design process, from concept generation and subsystem design to testing, validation, optimization, and final documentation.
