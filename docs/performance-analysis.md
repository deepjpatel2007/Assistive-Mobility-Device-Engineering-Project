# Performance Analysis

## Competition Results
The final vehicle achieved strong overall performance and demonstrated the effectiveness of the chosen mechanical and electrical architecture.

## Precision Speed Run

### Result
- Final Time: 5.81 seconds
- Final Score: 30/30

### Analysis
The speed run was the strongest aspect of the project. The low-mass chassis, front-wheel-drive system, pulley transmission, and Arduino timing strategy enabled the vehicle to achieve one of the fastest recorded performances.

### Observed Challenges
- Torque steer caused by offset pulley placement.
- Sensitivity to initial starting alignment.
- Minor timing adjustments required during competition testing.

## Eco-Trash Launch

### Result
- Final Score: 26/30

### Analysis
The launcher functioned successfully and consistently launched real trash into the scoring area. However, performance was lower than expected due to launcher arm deformation and inconsistencies in projectile release.

### Observed Challenges
- Launcher arm bending under tension.
- Servo interference with release mechanism.
- Projectile movement during vehicle travel.

## Mass Performance

### Results
- Measured Mass: 1148 g
- Calculated Mass: 1113 g

### Analysis
The vehicle remained under the 1200 g limit. Weight savings were achieved through a single-motor drivetrain, minimal hardware usage, and lightweight body materials.

## Safety Performance

All safety requirements were successfully satisfied:

- Static tipping test passed
- Upside-down test passed
- Line-of-sight requirement passed
- Teddy bear seating requirement passed

18.30 / 20

## Overall Assessment
The final project successfully balanced speed, stability, safety, cost, and manufacturability. The strongest subsystem was the mobility platform, while the launcher represented the largest opportunity for future improvement.
