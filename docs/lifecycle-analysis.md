# Lifecycle Analysis

## Overview
The lifecycle analysis evaluated the environmental impact of the Teddy Bear Wheelchair Project based on material use, production choices, electronics, and battery consumption.

## Reported Environmental Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Greenhouse Gas Emissions | 3263 g CO2e |
| Water Cyanide Contamination | 1.645 x 10^-1 g CN |
| Battery Consumption | Approximately two dozen AA batteries |

## Key Impact Areas

### Materials
The design used Meccano metal parts, cardboard, cardstock, wiring, electronics, and fasteners. The use of cardboard and paper-based body materials reduced mass and kept the exterior lightweight.

### Electronics
The Arduino, motor controller, DC motor, batteries, wiring, capacitors, resistors, and diodes contributed to the environmental footprint through material sourcing, manufacturing, and disposal considerations.

### Batteries
Battery consumption was one of the key environmental concerns. The final report notes that approximately two dozen AA batteries were used and regularly switched out during development and testing.

## Sustainability Considerations

The project prioritized performance and feasibility within a limited course timeline. For a future version, sustainability could be improved by:

- Using rechargeable batteries
- Reducing disposable battery waste
- Reusing structural components
- Reducing unnecessary purchased materials
- Designing a more efficient drivetrain
- Improving testing efficiency to reduce battery usage

## Future Improvements

A future version of the project could include a rechargeable lithium-ion or NiMH battery pack, reusable modular mounts, and a more efficient motor control strategy to reduce waste and improve long-term sustainability.
